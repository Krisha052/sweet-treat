# Beta Survey — Question Set

Copy these into a Google Form (Form title: "Sweet Treat Beta Feedback"). Keep it short — aim for under 3 minutes to complete, since response rate drops fast past that. Q1 (Overall rating) is the metric of record for reporting an average score; the rest are diagnostic, feeding into `BETA_RESULTS.md` themes and the design doc's risk/backlog items.

1. **Overall, how would you rate Sweet Treat?** *(Linear scale 1–5, 1 = Not fun, 5 = Really fun)* — required

2. **How far did you get?** *(Multiple choice: Didn't finish Level 1 / Finished Levels 1–3 / Finished Levels 4–6 / Finished all 8 levels)* — required

3. **How was the difficulty?** *(Linear scale 1–5, 1 = Too easy, 3 = Just right, 5 = Too hard)*

4. **Was it clear what to do without any tutorial?** *(Linear scale 1–5, 1 = Very confusing, 5 = Totally clear)*

5. **How did the art/visual style feel?** *(Linear scale 1–5, 1 = Didn't like it, 5 = Loved it)*

6. **Did anything crash, freeze, or visibly break?** *(Yes/No)*
   - If yes: **What happened, and on which level?** *(short answer)*

7. **What was the most confusing or frustrating moment?** *(paragraph, optional)*

8. **What did you enjoy most?** *(paragraph, optional)*

9. **If you could change one thing, what would it be?** *(paragraph, optional)*

10. **Would you keep playing if more levels were available?** *(Yes / No / Maybe)*

11. **What device did you play on?** *(short answer — e.g. "Pixel 7, Android 14" — used to correlate bug reports and the design doc's frame-rate/low-end-device claims with real hardware, not to identify anyone)*

12. **Anything else you want to mention?** *(paragraph, optional)*

---

**Form settings:**
- Collect responses into a linked Google Sheet (Responses tab → green Sheets icon) — that sheet is the raw data source for `BETA_RESULTS.md`.
- Turn off "Collect email addresses" unless you want to follow up individually — not needed for aggregate reporting.
- Enable "See summary charts and text responses" only for yourself (Form owner), not respondents.
